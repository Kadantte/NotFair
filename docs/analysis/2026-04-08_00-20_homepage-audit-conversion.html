So what
  1. 1 Debug the OAuth dropout (40% lost) — review /connect rage clicks and exception logs
    10 users started OAuth, 4 never finished. These are highest-intent users. 4 rage clicks on /connect confirm a specific UX blockage. Fixing this likely adds 2-3 accounts at current traffic levels with zero acquisition spend.
  2. 2 Drive traffic to /google-ads-audit, not just the homepage — test Reddit posts, Google Ads, SEO
    The audit page converts 6x better (50% vs 8.5% to /connect). Same user, dramatically different intent signal. A specific audit offer ('see your score') outperforms the broad homepage pitch. More traffic here = more conversions per dollar.
  3. 3 Add CTA click tracking to both pages ('Audit Now' button → track as cta_clicked with page property)
    Currently blind to what happens between pageview and /connect. Can't know if 50% audit→connect is due to button placement, message, or self-selection. One tracking call unblocks all future A/B testing on CTAs.
  4. 4 Add mobile email capture on home page — 'Get setup guide sent to your desktop'
    39% of home page visitors (29 users) are on mobile and structurally can't convert to CLI. Capturing email recovers this otherwise-lost traffic. Even 10% email capture on mobile = 3 leads/week at current traffic.
