Landing page is attracting new visitors but converting almost none — 5 accounts from 79 unique visitors in 4 days
"Is the landing page converting visitors into new customers?"
Bottom line
You are getting new visitors — 71 first-time users since launch (Apr 4). But only 5 connected a Google Ads account (6.3% of visitors), and traffic is entirely dependent on one-off spikes (Reddit, email blast). There is no sustainable acquisition channel yet, and 36% of traffic is mobile where conversion is impossible (CLI product). The good news: today (Apr 7) shows a recovery to 15 visitors with 2 new account connections, and users who do convert are highly engaged (55 AI changes per active user).

Why
  • Traffic crashed 90% after launch day then partially recovered High
    51 visitors on Apr 4, dropped to 5 and 8, recovered to 15 today. Launch spike was Reddit (14 user-days) and email blast (9 user-days) — neither repeatable. Google organic is only 4 user-days.
  • 5 users connected accounts — conversion rate improved to 6.3% Medium
    Up from 2% (1/48) in the prior analysis. New connections are happening daily (1, 1, 1, 2), suggesting the /connect funnel works for motivated users who reach it. Small sample (n=5)
  • 36% of traffic is mobile with zero conversion path High
    30 of 84 device-sessions are mobile. Product is CLI-only — these users see a landing page and bounce. Mobile traffic comes from Reddit/social where mobile browsing dominates.
  • 90% of visitors are first-time — almost no one returns High
    71 of 79 unique visitors are new. The 8 returning users are likely internal. No retention mechanism for visitors who don't convert on first visit.
  • Active users are highly engaged — 55 changes per user High
    220 AI changes and 401 AI reads from ~4 active users. Users who convert are getting real, repeated value from the product.
  • Homepage bounce is the biggest funnel leak — 84% drop off High
    75 unique users hit homepage, only 13 reach /connect (17%). The homepage → deeper page transition loses 60% of visitors.

So what
  1. 1 **Build a repeatable acquisition channel** — Reddit and email gave a one-time spike. Google organic is 4 user-days. Invest in SEO content targeting 'Google Ads MCP', 'Google Ads AI agent', 'manage Google Ads with Claude' — zero-competition terms where you can rank fast. Expected impact: 5-15 organic visitors/day within 4-6 weeks.
  2. 2 **Reduce homepage bounce for desktop users** — 84% of visitors never leave the homepage. Add a compelling demo, live example of AI managing ads, or 'see it in action' above the fold. Focus desktop. Expected impact: could double /connect visits from 13 to 25+.
  3. 3 **Add mobile email capture** — 36% of traffic can't convert. Add 'Get setup instructions emailed to your desktop' for mobile visitors. Lowest effort, highest ROI way to recover mobile traffic.
  4. 4 **Celebrate: users who convert love it** — 5 active users generated 621 AI actions in 4 days. Distribution is the bottleneck, not product quality.
